About the Project

This project utilizes satellite and model-based datasets to map greenhouse gas emissions, offering insights into both human-caused and natural sources. Explore data that informs climate policy and decision-making by visualizing sources and sinks of emissions globally.

  • Data-driven approach to understanding emissions
  • Mapping human-caused and natural greenhouse gas emissions
  • Empowering policymakers with accurate data

Why Understanding Greenhouse Gases Matters

Human-caused and natural greenhouse gas emissions significantly contribute to climate change. Understanding how different sources and sinks interplay is essential to devising strategies that curb emissions and protect the environment. The GHG Mapping Project brings these dynamics into focus through high-quality data that helps stakeholders make informed decisions.

  • 01 How do human activities impact greenhouse gas emissions?

    Human activities, including fossil fuel combustion, industrial processes, and agriculture, are the largest contributors to rising GHG levels. These emissions enhance the natural greenhouse effect, leading to global temperature increases and extreme weather events.

  • 02 What role do natural carbon sinks play?

    Natural carbon sinks, such as forests and oceans, absorb nearly half of all CO2 emissions produced by human activity. However, their capacity is diminishing due to deforestation, ocean acidification, and other environmental stressors.

  • 03 How can data from the GHG Mapping Project help?

    Our data provides actionable insights into the global distribution of GHG sources and sinks. By offering granular data on emissions and absorption, our platform equips researchers and policymakers with the tools needed to address climate change head-on.
